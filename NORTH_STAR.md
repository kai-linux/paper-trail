# North Star

**Make it impossible for an LLM to lie about a research paper.**

Every claim paper-trail produces is grounded in a specific span of a specific PDF, on a specific page, in a specific section — and the user can click through to verify in one second.

## Who it's for

Researchers, PhD students, engineers, and curious readers who want to interrogate a corpus of papers conversationally without surrendering trust. People who have been burned by a confidently-wrong AI summary of a paper they cared about.

## What "done" feels like

> A user drops 50 PDFs into paper-trail, asks *"how does RAPTOR's hierarchical clustering compare to GraphRAG's community detection?"*, and gets a 4-paragraph answer where every sentence has a citation that opens the exact paragraph in the source PDF when clicked. Zero hallucination. Zero unsourced claims.

## Single sentence

**Conversational research, with receipts.**

## What we will not become

- A general-purpose document chatbot
- A knowledge graph product
- A SaaS with a paywall
- A wrapper that just calls `pdf-to-text → stuff in context window`

Stay narrow. Stay local-first. Stay honest about provenance.
